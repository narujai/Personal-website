About Me

Hi! Welcome to my website. I am Naru Jai, a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech. My expertise is in developing scalable and real-time algorithms for optimizing radio resource allocation and compliance-aware network performance to address practical spectrum-sharing and RF coexistence challenges in next-generation wireless systems across commercial and federal bands.

I have worked on coexistence problems in the Citizens Broadband Radio Service (CBRS) band, Automated Frequency Coordination (AFC)-controlled 6 GHz access, and active-passive spectrum sharing, including 5G out-of-band emission modeling for radio astronomy protection.

My industry experience includes an internship at Federated Wireless, where I worked on incumbent-protection algorithms and methodologies for the 3 GHz and CBRS bands.

I am currently exploring new opportunities across academia and industry. Please feel free to reach out at naru@vt.edu to connect, discuss potential opportunities, or learn more about my work.
